What is Roller Derby?


Roller Derby is a contact sport played on roller skates.

Two teams of five players skate on an oval track, with each team designating one player as the "jammer" who scores points by lapping members of the opposing team.

The other players, called "blockers," use their bodies to block the opposing jammer and help their own jammer score points.

Points are also scored by the jammer if they pass members of the opposing team.

The game consists of multiple "jams" in which both teams send their jammer out to score points, and is typically a very physical and fast-paced sport.

History


Originally Roller Derby was a marathon on skates, played by two competing teams with both men and women on each team.

It was played on a banked track.

It was started in the 1930s by Leo Seltzer and Damon Runyon in the US.

It gradually became a form of "sports entertainment" with scripted bout and predetermined winners, where the focus was on spectacle and not sport.

This version of Roller Derby died out in the US during the early 1970s.

Rebirth in the 2000s


Roller Derby was reborn in Texas in the early 2000s.

After the initial failure of Daniel Eduardo Policarpo, who formed a production company called "Bad Girl Goood Woman Productions", to promote the sport.

However a large number of players for this promotion were unhappy with the managmement of it, left to form their team that ultimately became Texas Roller Girls.

From there the sport began to grow and this then lead to the creation of the WFTDA in 2005 to promote and regualte the new sport of Roller Derby.
